What to Do Right Now

  • 1
    Call a rescuer: phone only, no texts

    If you have any concerns for its health, safety or location, call immediately and stay with the koala. Do not text or Facebook message: calls only so we can respond fast.

    phone
    0424 807 470 Koala Rescue Brisbane South (primary)
    phone
    0438 075 156 John Knights
    phone
    1300 264 625 RSPCA (1300ANIMAL)
  • 2
    Stay with the koala and observe

    Remain nearby but keep pets and people back. When you call, be ready to share:

    • Exact location: street address, GPS pin, or a clear landmark
    • Where is the koala: on the ground, or up a tree? How high?
    • Is it awake and moving, or curled up asleep?
    • What's around it: quiet street, busy road, dogs, or crowds?
    • Your reason for concern: injury, wet fur, acting strangely?
  • 3
    A rescuer will assess and advise

    Based on what you tell us, we'll determine the right course of action and advise you on next steps. Your observations are what guides our response: every detail matters.

Signs a Koala Needs Help

Koalas should be in trees. If you notice any of the following, call us immediately.

Call a rescuer if you notice:

  • water_drop
    Bottom is wet, dirty or brown
  • visibility
    Eyes are red, inflamed or crusty
  • accessibility_new
    Body is thin: spine or hip bones are visible
  • arrow_circle_down
    Sitting low in a tree or on the ground
  • calendar_month
    In the same tree for 3–4 days or more
  • healing
    Injuries visible: cuts, blood, lost fur, or not using all 4 limbs
  • child_care
    Orphan joey: smaller than a pineapple and not near mum
  • directions_car
    Any contact with a car or dog, even if it looks okay
  • crisis_alert
    At risk: near a road, no habitat nearby, or looks lost
  • help
    You aren't sure: when in doubt, call us
shield Safety first: Don't touch the koala, even if it seems friendly. Injured animals can bite and scratch. Keep your distance and call us.

Who We Are

Real People, Real Work

We're over 100 volunteers from Brisbane South: tree surgeons, nurses, students, and retirees, united by one mission: save injured koalas.

Founded by John Knights with over 20 years of wildlife rescue experience, KRBS combines professional expertise with genuine community care.

We don't just rescue. We rehabilitate, reunite animals with their habitat, and keep learning from every single rescue.
