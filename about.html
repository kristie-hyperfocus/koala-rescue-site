John's Vision

John Knights has spent over 20 years in wildlife rescue and rehabilitation. Holding a permit from the Queensland Department of Environment, Science and Innovation, he founded Koala Rescue Brisbane South with a simple belief: every injured koala deserves professional, compassionate care: no matter the time, the weather, or how hard it is to reach them.

What started as one person with a mission has grown into something remarkable. A growing team of dedicated local volunteers, each bringing unique skills and genuine passion, now responds to koalas in crisis across Brisbane South.

John mentors every volunteer personally. He believes the best rescuers are made, not found: and he's committed to building a team that will outlast any single individual.

How We Work

When someone calls, we respond. 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Trained volunteers arrive with the right equipment, the right knowledge, and the right attitude to get the job done safely.

But we don't just rescue. We assess, we rehabilitate, and we reunite animals with their habitat. Every rescue is documented. Every case is a chance to learn.

The People Behind the Mission

Our volunteers are coordinators, first responders, tree climbers, trap sitters, transporters, and community advocates. But they're not just titles: they're local people who care.

From the person who spots an injured koala on their morning walk to the rescuer who drives an hour at midnight: everyone plays a part. We celebrate each other, support each other, and make decisions together.

We gather regularly under the trees for training sessions and team building: staying sharp, staying connected, and staying ready.
