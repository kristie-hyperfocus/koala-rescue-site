KNOW THE SIGNS

How to Help a Koala

Knowing what to look for—and what to do—can save a koala's life.

⚠️ Signs a Koala Needs Help
💧
Wet/dirty bottomCystitis or chlamydia
👀
Red or crusty eyesMay indicate conjunctivitis
🦼
Thin bodySpine or bones visible
⬇️
Sitting low or on groundNot normal behaviour
📅
Same tree 3–4 daysMay be stuck or unwell
🩸
Visible injuriesCuts, blood, lost fur, limping
👶
Orphaned joeySmaller than a pineapple, alone
🚧
At riskNear cars/dogs, looks lost
🚗
Car or dog contactEven if they seem fine
❓
Not sure?When in doubt, call us

Koalas may walk or climb with injuries that aren't visible—they always need expert assessment.

✅ What To Do
1
Keep your distance

Stay 10+ metres away. Don't touch the koala.

2
Note the location

Street address, cross street, or GPS coordinates.

📞
CALL a rescuer

Don't text! They may not see it & time is critical.

4
Stay and wait

Keep the koala in sight so rescuers can find it.

5
Keep dogs away

Slow traffic if near a road and it's safe to do so.

⏰ Daily risk: Late afternoon to mid-morning
📅 Peak season: July to December (breeding)

Simple Ways to Help Every Day

🚗
Drive Carefully

Slow down from late afternoon to mid-morning, especially near bush areas.

🐶
Contain Dogs

Keep dogs inside at night and on a lead in public near koala habitat.

🌳
Plant Natives

Retain and plant native trees to protect koala habitat in your area.

📱
Report Sightings

Use the QWildlife app to log sightings and help conservation efforts.
